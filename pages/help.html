Getting Started

Let’s Code allows you to practice coding through Rapid Fire questions and full coding challenges.

1.Difficulty Levels:
All topics across each programming language are organized in ascending order, beginning with basic concepts and progressing to advanced subtopics.
Users may follow the structured order for guided learning or select any topic of their choice to begin practicing.
This flexible approach allows learners to either build foundational knowledge step by step or focus on specific areas of interest.


Rapid Fire Mode

Timed conceptual or MCQ-based questions to test your speed and accuracy.


2.Rapid Fire Rules:
! Each topic within a selected programming language includes a Rapid Fire practice option.
! The Rapid Fire session consists of five questions per topic.
! Each session is conducted within a specified time limit [30 seconds].
! Users must answer all five questions within the allotted time for the score to be considered valid.
! If all five questions are not completed within the time limit, the score will not be recorded.
! Upon successfully answering all five questions, the final score will be displayed immediately.
[This feature is designed to improve speed, accuracy, and conceptual understanding under timed conditions.]


Coding Challenges

Write code in the built-in editor and submit solutions evaluated against test cases.

3.Coding Rules:
! Each topic includes a Coding Practice section containing two coding questions.
Submission policy: ! Users are allowed a total of four submission attempts per visit for each topic.
! During the first two attempts, users are expected to solve the problem independently.
! If the correct solution is not achieved after the first two attempts, a hint or reference solution will be provided via a pop-up window.
! After reviewing the hint, users will receive two additional submission attempts to solve the problem independently.
! If the solution remains incorrect after all four attempts, the user will be redirected to the topic page to review the learning material before attempting again.
[This structure encourages independent problem-solving while still providing guided support when necessary.]

Account & Profile

Account Security & Access Password Management
Update Your Credentials: You can change your password at any time through the Profile Settings menu to keep your account secure.
Stay Unique: We strongly recommend using a unique password that you don't use on other sites to ensure your personal progress and streak data stay safe.
Session Management
Automatic Login: Our dashboard uses PHP Sessions to remember who you are while you move between pages.
Identifying "User": If your dashboard displays "Welcome, User" instead of your actual name, your session may have expired. Simply log out and log back in to re-authenticate and see your personalized stats.
Account Recovery
Lost Access?: If you are unable to log in, please use the "Forgot Password" link on the login page.
Restore Your Data: Once you verify your identity, the system will automatically reconnect you to your existing user_id, restoring your full history of solved questions and your current learning streak.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Let’s Code free?
Yes, basic practice is free.
Can I retake questions?
Yes, you can practice again anytime.
How are topics organized on the platform?
All topics across each programming language are arranged in ascending order, starting from basic concepts and progressing to advanced subtopics. However, users are free to choose any topic and begin practicing at any time.
Do I have to complete topics in order?
No. While the topics are structured progressively for better learning, you may select any topic based on your preference or current skill level.
What is Rapid Fire mode?
Rapid Fire mode is a timed practice session available for each topic. It consists of five questions designed to test your speed and conceptual understanding.
How many questions are included in Rapid Fire mode?
Each Rapid Fire session contains five questions related to the selected topic.
Is there a time limit for Rapid Fire?
Yes. Rapid Fire mode operates within a specified time limit. All five questions must be answered within the allotted time.
What happens if I do not complete all five Rapid Fire questions?
If all five questions are not answered within the time limit, the score will not be recorded.
When will I see my Rapid Fire score?
Your score will be displayed immediately after you successfully complete all five questions within the given time.
How many attempts do I get to submit my code?
You are allowed a total of four submission attempts per visit for each topic.
What happens during the first two coding attempts?
During the first two attempts, you are expected to solve the problem independently without any assistance.
Will I receive help if I cannot solve the coding problem?
Yes. If your solution is incorrect after the first two attempts, a hint or reference solution will be provided in a pop-up window to guide you.
What happens after I view the hint?
After reviewing the hint, you will receive two additional submission attempts to solve the problem on your own.
What happens if I still cannot solve the coding problem after four attempts?
If the solution remains incorrect after all four attempts, you will be redirected to the topic page to review the learning material before trying again.
Can I try the coding questions again later?
Yes. You may revisit the topic and attempt the coding questions again in a new session.
Why is there a limit on submission attempts?
The submission limit encourages focused problem-solving, independent thinking, and effective use of hints while maintaining structured learning.
